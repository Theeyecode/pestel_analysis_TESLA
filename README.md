### **2. Trade & Economic Activity**
- Europe, Oceania, and North America lead in **GDP per capita**  
- Asia dominates **global trade volume & value**  
- Top trading countries relevant to Tesla include **Singapore, Netherlands, UK, Canada**

**Interpretation:**  
Tesla‚Äôs supply chain is inherently tied to Asia for raw materials and Europe/North America for high-value manufacturing and markets.

---

### **3. Environmental & Socioeconomic Indicators**
- CO‚ÇÇ emissions highest in industrial regions (South America mining sites highlighted)  
- Electricity access strongly correlated with EV adoption potential  
- Government effectiveness highest in Europe & Oceania  

**Insight:**  
Regions with both high electricity access and high emissions represent Tesla‚Äôs strongest market opportunity ‚Äî a combination of need and readiness.

---

### **4. EV Battery Material & Adoption Trends**
- BEV sales (2010‚Äì2020): **China leads**, followed by Europe & U.S.  
- Lithium and nickel usage spike post-2017  
- Cobalt usage remains volatile, increasing supply chain risk  
- EV stock share grew exponentially during the decade  

**Insight:**  
Upstream pressure on lithium and nickel will intensify as EV adoption accelerates globally.

## üìå Key Insights & Findings

### **1. Political Stability Shapes Supply Chain Risk**
Europe and North America score the highest in governance effectiveness, making them attractive zones for stable long-term operations.  
Africa and parts of Asia introduce geopolitical risk.

### **2. Economic Strength Predicts Market Potential**
High-income nations with strong purchasing power (EU, North America, Oceania) remain Tesla‚Äôs most viable consumer markets.

### **3. Environmental Pressures Drive EV Momentum**
Regions with high CO‚ÇÇ emissions but strong electricity access show the fastest EV adoption ‚Äî aligning with Tesla‚Äôs value proposition.

### **4. Battery Material Dependency Is a Strategic Vulnerability**
Rapid growth in lithium and nickel usage signals:
- Higher future procurement cost  
- Increased exposure to supply chain shocks  
- Need for battery innovation or recycling solutions
